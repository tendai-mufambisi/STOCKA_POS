🔑Activation

When you first launch Stocka you will be prompted to enter a licence key before the app opens. Your key looks like this:

XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXX
  • Enter your licence key in the activation screen. Dashes are added automatically as you type.
  • Click Activate. The key is verified locally — no internet is needed.
  • Once accepted, the app proceeds to the Shop Setup wizard (first time) or the login screen.
Keep your key safe. You will need it if you reinstall the application or move to a new computer.

🏪Shop Setup

The first time Stocka starts after activation, a four-step wizard walks you through the initial configuration.

Step 1 — Shop Details

Enter your shop name, address, and contact number. These appear on receipts.

Step 2 — Currency

Choose your trading currency (e.g. USD, ZWG). This is used throughout the app.

Step 3 — Owner PIN

Set a 4-digit PIN for the owner account. This is the Admin account — keep it private.

Step 4 — First Product (optional)

Add your first product to get started, or skip and add products later.

Tip: You can change all of these settings later in Settings → Shop Details.

🔐Logging In

After setup, every session begins at the PIN login screen.

  • Select your name from the user list on screen.
  • Enter your 4-digit PIN on the keypad.
  • The app opens at the Dashboard.
Forgot your PIN? An Admin or Manager can reset it in Settings → Users.

📊Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home screen. It gives you a live snapshot of today's business at a glance.

What you can see

Today's Sales

Total revenue and number of transactions made today.

Low-Stock Alerts

Products that have fallen below their reorder level.

Expense Summary

Total expenses logged today vs. this week/month.

Active Cashier

Manager/Admin view: see which cashiers have open shifts right now.

Quick Actions

The Dashboard contains shortcut buttons to the most common tasks — making a sale, receiving stock, and logging an expense — so you don't have to navigate through menus every time.

New to Stocka? An onboarding checklist appears on the Dashboard until you complete the key setup steps (add products, add a cashier, do your first sale).

🛒Making a Sale

The Sales screen is your point-of-sale register. Any logged-in user can process sales.

Processing a transaction

  • Search for a product by name or scan its barcode into the search box.
  • Click the product to add it to the cart. Adjust quantity as needed.
  • Repeat for all items the customer is buying.
  • Click Charge and enter the cash the customer hands over.
  • The app calculates change. Click Confirm to complete the sale.
  • A receipt is printed automatically if a printer is connected.

Held Sales

If a customer needs to step away mid-transaction, use the Hold button to park the current cart. Retrieve held sales from the hold queue and continue later.

Important: Held sales do not reserve stock. Another cashier can sell the same items while a sale is on hold.

Reprinting a receipt

Go to Reports → Daily Sales, find the transaction, and click the print icon next to it.

🌙End of Day

The End of Day process reconciles your cash drawer at the end of each trading day. Manager Admin

  • Navigate to End of Day from the sidebar.
  • Count the physical cash in your drawer.
  • Enter the counted amount into the Actual Cash field.
  • Stocka compares this against expected cash (opening float + sales − expenses).
  • Review any variance. A green status means balanced; red means there is a discrepancy.
  • Click Close Day to finalise the session.

When multiple cashiers work in the same day, End of Day shows a per-cashier breakdown so you can see where any variance occurred.

Best practice: Do End of Day every trading day before closing. Skipping days makes reconciliation harder.

💸Expenses

Log any money spent running the business — rent, salaries, electricity, delivery costs, etc.

Adding an expense

  • Go to Expenses in the sidebar.
  • Click Add Expense.
  • Select a category, enter the amount, date, and an optional note.
  • Click Save.

Expenses are deducted in the End of Day cash reconciliation, so record them on the day they are paid.

Viewing expense history

Use the day / week / month tabs and the search bar to filter past expenses. The summary card at the top shows totals for the selected period.

📦Products

Products are the items you sell. Everything in Stocka — sales, stock levels, reorder alerts — is built around your product list.

Adding a product

  • Go to Products in the sidebar.
  • Click Add Product.
  • Fill in the product name, selling price, cost price, category, and reorder level.
  • Optionally assign a supplier and add a product image.
  • Click Save.

Key fields explained

FieldWhat it does
Cost PriceWhat you paid per unit. Used to calculate profit in reports.
Selling PriceWhat the customer pays.
Reorder LevelWhen stock falls to this number, Stocka alerts you in the Dashboard and Restock Needed page.
CategoryGroups products for filtering and reporting.
SupplierWho supplies this product. Links to the Suppliers page.

Editing & deleting

Click any product row to open its edit form. To delete a product, open it and click Delete. Products with sales history are archived rather than permanently deleted.

Exporting the product list

Click Export to Excel at the top of the Products page to download a spreadsheet of your full product catalogue.

📥Receiving Stock

Whenever new stock arrives — from a supplier or a direct purchase — record it here so inventory levels stay accurate.

  • Go to Stock Control in the sidebar.
  • Click Receive Stock.
  • Select the product, enter the quantity received, cost per unit, and date.
  • Optionally select the supplier this stock came from.
  • Click Save. The product's stock level updates immediately.

The Purchase History tab below shows all past stock receives, searchable by product or supplier.

Tip: Recording cost per unit each time you receive stock lets Stocka track your actual cost basis even when supplier prices change.

📋Current Inventory

A real-time snapshot of every product's current stock quantity and total value.

Use the column headers to sort by name, quantity, or value. Use the search bar to find a specific product quickly. Products below their reorder level are highlighted in red.

Click Export CSV to download the inventory list — useful for stocktake records or sharing with an accountant.

⚠️Restock Needed

This page lists every product that has fallen below its reorder level, ranked by urgency.

UrgencyMeaning
CriticalStock is at zero or will run out within a day or two based on sales velocity.
HighLess than a week of stock remaining at the current sales rate.
MediumBelow reorder level but not immediately urgent.

Each row shows current stock, the reorder shortfall, average daily sales (last 30 days), and estimated days until stockout. Use this page to prioritise what to order first.

💀Dead Stock Analysis

Dead stock is inventory that has not sold in a long time and is tying up your cash. This page surfaces those products.

Use the days filter (14 / 30 / 60 / 90 days) to adjust the definition of "not moving." The total value of dead stock is shown at the top so you can see how much cash is locked up.

Suggested actions include running a promotion, bundling with fast-moving items, returning to supplier, or discounting to clear.

📅Expiry Tracking

If you sell perishable goods, this page helps you stay on top of products approaching or past their expiry date.

Recording expiry dates

Expiry dates are recorded when stock arrives: in Stock Control → Record Stock, fill in the optional Expiry Date field next to Date Received (it works for both supplier receivings and direct purchases, and the Excel import sheet accepts an Expiry Date column too). Each receiving with an expiry date becomes a tracked batch on this page — leave the field blank for non-perishables.

Status categories

⚠️ Expiring Soon

Expires within the next 7 days. Consider marking down for quick sale.

🔴 Expired

Past expiry date. Should be discarded or returned to supplier immediately.

Admins and Managers can use the Discard / Remove button on each batch to write off the units being thrown away (the write-off is capped at stock on hand and logged as a stock movement) and clear the batch from tracking. Enter 0 units to clear a batch without changing stock — for example when it already sold out.

Legal note: Selling expired products is illegal. Use this page regularly if you stock food, medicine, or any dated goods.

🔄Inventory Reconciliation

A physical stocktake lets you compare what the system says you have versus what is actually on the shelf. Do this periodically to catch errors, theft, or unrecorded damage.

  • Go to Inventory Reconciliation in the sidebar.
  • Physically count every product in your store.
  • Enter your counted quantities into the Counted column next to each product.
  • The app shows discrepancies (plus or minus) automatically.
  • Add notes to explain large variances (damaged goods, supplier short-delivery, etc.).
  • Click Finalise to update inventory levels to your counted figures.

You can export the reconciliation report as CSV to keep a paper trail.

Best practice: Do a full stocktake at least once a month. For high-value or high-theft items, consider weekly counts.

🚚Suppliers

Suppliers are the businesses or individuals you buy your products from. Linking products to suppliers makes reordering and tracking easier.

Adding a supplier

  • Go to Suppliers in the sidebar.
  • Click Add Supplier.
  • Enter the supplier's name, phone number, email, and address.
  • Click Save.

Each supplier card shows the total number of products they supply and the combined stock value of those products — useful for knowing your dependency on each supplier.

👥Shifts & Cashier Sessions

Manager Admin

Every time a cashier logs in, a new shift session is created. Their sales are tracked against that session, which makes cash reconciliation straightforward even when multiple cashiers work in a day.

Shift Dashboard

Shows all shifts — open and closed — with status, duration, total sales, and cash balance. Filter by open/closed or by date to find a specific shift. Click a shift to see its full detail and reconciliation status.

Cashier Sessions

The Cashier Sessions page is a live view: it shows currently active sessions (who is logged in right now) and allows you to view historical sessions with filters for cashier name, reconciliation status, and date range.

Auto-refresh: The live session view refreshes automatically so you always see the current state of the shop floor.

🌐LAN / Multi-Computer Setup

Stocka can run across multiple computers on the same WiFi network — for example, one computer at the till and one at the manager's desk.

Modes

Standalone

Default mode. The app runs on one computer only, with no network sync.

Server

This computer holds the main database. All other computers sync to this one.

Client

A satellite terminal (e.g. a cashier till). It connects to the Server computer over WiFi.

Setting up a Server + Client pair

  • On the main computer: open LAN Settings and switch to Server mode. Note the IP address shown.
  • On the till computer: open LAN Settings and switch to Client mode.
  • Click Discover to find the server automatically, or enter the IP address manually.
  • Click Connect. The client will sync with the server database.
Both computers must be on the same WiFi network. This feature does not work over the internet.

👤Users & Roles

Admin  Manage who can log in and what they can do in Settings → Users.

Roles

RoleAccess Level
Admin Full access to everything including settings, user management, reports, and all financial data.
Manager Access to reports, shifts, End of Day, and day-to-day operations. Cannot change settings or manage users.
Cashier Can process sales, log expenses, and receive stock. Cannot view reports or manage the shop.

Adding a user

  • Go to Settings → Users.
  • Click Add User.
  • Enter their name, choose a role, and set a 4-digit PIN.
  • Click Save. They can log in immediately.

Resetting a PIN

Find the user in the list, click Edit, and enter a new PIN. The user will need to use the new PIN at their next login.

Deactivating a user

If a staff member leaves, deactivate their account rather than deleting it. Their historical sales data is preserved. Deactivated users cannot log in.

💾Backups

Stocka stores all data locally on your computer. If the hard drive fails or the computer is lost, you lose all your data — unless you have a backup.

Back up regularly. Stocka cannot recover data without a backup file.

Creating a backup

  • Go to Settings → Database Backup.
  • Click Create Backup.
  • Choose a save location — ideally a USB drive or a cloud-synced folder (Dropbox, Google Drive).
  • A .db backup file is saved to that location.

Restoring from a backup

  • Go to Settings → Database Backup.
  • Click Restore Backup.
  • Select your backup .db file.
  • The app will restart and load data from the backup.
Recommendation: Back up at the end of every working day. Copy the backup to a USB drive or upload it to Google Drive so it is stored off-site.
